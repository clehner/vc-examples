A customer loyalty card is traditionally a plastic card given to a customer by a retailer. Scanned at point of sale, it contains a persistent identifier that the retailer uses to identify the customer and correlate their purchases over time. It may be associated with an online account and/or digital coupons issued to the customer.

The vocabulary terms that appear in this document and the associated JSON-LD context file have not been reviewed for standards-track adoption by any entity. They serve primarily as an example of how a customer loyalty credential may function and are ready for review by appropriate communities and groups.

CustomerLoyaltyCredential

Terms that are used in a CustomerLoyaltyCredential.credentialSubject
Term id Term name Term description
customerLoyaltyCard customer loyalty card Describes a customer loyalty card issued to the credential subject.

CustomerLoyaltyCard

A CustomerLoyaltyCard defines the identifier associated with a customer, and often a branch or location code for the store that is the customer's preferred branch in a retail chain at which to shop.

Terms that make up a CustomerLoyaltyCard included in credentialSubject.customerLoyaltyCard.
Term id Term name Term description
identifier Identifier The primary identifier of the customer, by this retailer (issuer). It may be a number, UUID, or other string.
branchCode Branch code An identifier for the branch or location used by the issuer to identify a location at which the customer typically shops.
